Market Signal

Japan is the #1 AI-planned destination

GuideGeek (Matador Network's AI travel tool) reports Japan as the most-requested itinerary destination globally — ahead of Italy, Thailand, and France.18 This means Tripwheel's AI-draft feature has a ready-made use case.

Tourism Boom

Record-breaking arrivals and spending

Japan received 61.5M visitors in 2024 — surpassing the pre-COVID peak of 31.9M (2019) by nearly 2×.16 Tourist spending hit ¥8.1 trillion (≈$53B), up 48% year-over-year. The government targets 100M visitors by 2030.

Planning Complexity

Japan is uniquely hard to plan

Japan's transit system is world-class but opaque to visitors:

  • Transit confusion: Tokyo alone has 13 metro lines + JR + private railways.
  • Neighbourhood density: A single day can cover 5+ distinct areas. Geographic planning is critical.
  • Reservation culture: Many top restaurants and experiences require advance booking — often in Japanese only.
  • Seasonal timing: Cherry blossom, autumn leaves, and festival dates shift yearly.
  • Language barrier: English signage exists but is inconsistent. Offline maps are essential.
Behaviour

How Japan travellers actually plan

  • Average planning time: 40+ hours over 2–4 weeks for a 7-day Japan trip.20
  • Top tools used: Google Maps (transit), Reddit r/JapanTravel (advice), YouTube (visual research), Notion/Sheets (organisation).
  • Common structure: Tokyo (3–4 days) → day trip → Kyoto (2–3 days) → Osaka (1–2 days).
  • Group dynamics: Japan trips are frequently planned by one person and shared via screenshots or Notion pages.
Opportunity

Why Japan = perfect Tripwheel launch

Japan trips combine every pain point the research identified:

Complex transit Neighbourhood-dense Reservation-dependent Seasonal timing Language barrier High willingness to plan Strong sharing culture AI-planned #1 destination

A tool that solves Japan planning solves every planning problem. If Tripwheel can make a 7-day Tokyo itinerary in 2 minutes — with maps, transit times, offline access, and a shareable link — it proves the concept for every other destination.

"Japan is the ultimate stress test for itinerary tools. If it works for Tokyo, it works everywhere."— Product strategy implication from this research

Limitations

What this research doesn't cover

  • Quantitative user research: No original surveys or interviews were conducted.
  • Non-English sources: Japanese forums and app reviews were not reviewed.
  • Pricing/willingness-to-pay: No data on what travellers would pay for a premium tool.
  • Mobile vs. desktop split: The planning-device breakdown is not sourced.
  • Conversion data: No data on itinerary-to-booking conversion.
